Paris is RED



Manchester United have finally qualified for the 20/21 UEFA Champions League after a tumultous 19/20 Premier League season. There were many doubts but United finished 3rd to qualify for the competition once again. This time around, Manchester United will be drawn against 3 other teams in the group stages of the competition.

As fate would have it once again, Manchester United have been drawn against Paris Saint-Germain in the group of death. The other 2 teams in the group were RB Leipzig and İstanbul Başakşehir F.K. Manchester United's group have been dubbed the group of death as RB Leipzig was the semi-finalist of last season's edition. Following the heartbreak of the 2018/19 competition, Paris Saint-Germain have progressed to the finals of the 2019/20 edition, however, they fell short of football's most prestigious price, losing to FC Bayern München.





The first game of the 20/21 will be Paris Saint-Germain vs Manchester United at Paris once again. This time round, Manchester United are heading into the game with an almost full strength squad. Majority of the squad was the same as the 18/19 season but United have bolstered their team with the signing of Portugese maestro, Bruno Fernandes. Manchester United fans were not exactly confident of a victory but were much more hopeful as compared to 19 months ago.

And United started the game well. Manchester United were awarded a penalty once again at the 23rd minute after a rough tackle by Paris's defender. Bruno stepped up and missed the penalty after a brilliant save by Keylor Navas but the referee has seen an infringement by the Paris's keeper and has ordered for a retake. And this time round, Bruno Fernandes makes no mistake.





Manchester United went into the half time break with a 1-0 lead and things were looking rosy. United have started off the second half brighly once again. However, in the 55th minute, Neymar's corner for Paris was converted by Manchester United's very own striker, Anthony Martial. It was quite a header.





As time went on, the match looked destined to be a draw. In the 87th minute, however, there was to be a hero once again. And who else but Manchester United's very own Marcus Rashford once again. However, this time round, his full name is Marcus Rashford MBE. Marcus Rashford was bestowed with the title of the Member of the British Empire for his work in securing food vouchers for the poor kids in United Kingdom and his continual fight to end child food poverty. The issue of child food poverty was exacerbated by the COVID-19 situation and many kids were about to go hungry if not for his intervention. What. An. Absolute. Hero. For the Manchester United fans and the British people.





Well, does the MBE in Rashford's name stand for Member of British Empire or Must Be Elite? I will let you think about that.





Manchester United is set to face PSG once again on the 3rd of December. Will Rashford step up once again for Manchester United to win it? Or will Ángel Di María have the last say? We shall see. Thank you for watching!
